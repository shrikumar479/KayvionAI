"Making the best bread is the ultimate demonstration of the craft of food preparation and production."

The Flour Pot Bakery was founded by Oli Hyde — a Brighton restaurant chef and caterer who believed that exceptional baking was the purest expression of the culinary craft. From the beginning, the mission was simple: make the finest bread, use the best ingredients, and never cut corners.

We've been baking in Brighton since 2012, maintaining the same sourdough starter since the day we opened. Every loaf and every laminated pastry is made by hand in our bakehouse. As we've expanded across the city — twelve locations and counting — we've never automated our baking process. We simply adapted our shifts to retain the craftsmanship.

As a family-run Brighton business, supporting local and serving the highest quality food is at the heart of everything we do. Our coffee is roasted just down the road by our sister company, The Milk Shed, in Kemptown. We partner with City Food Share and Too Good To Go to ensure nothing goes to waste.

Made by Hand

No factory finishes. No automation. Every loaf is hand-shaped, every pastry hand-laminated. We've expanded by hiring bakers, not machines.

Honest Ingredients

Real butter. Free-range eggs. Proper vanilla. Our sourdoughs contain no baker's yeast — just time, flour, water, and a thirteen-year-old starter.

Rooted in Brighton

We bake here, roast here, and serve here. Our Steam Tube Polin Oven runs on wood pellets — one of only three in the UK — and has cut our energy use by over 30%.
